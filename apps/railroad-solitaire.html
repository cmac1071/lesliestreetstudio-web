How it works

A game of alignment and chain reactions

52 cards are dealt one by one into a single row. Your success depends entirely on the relationship between the 1st and 4th cards from the right end of the row.

Rank match

Same rank — clear four

When the 1st and 4th cards from the right share the same rank — two Kings, two Fives — all four cards in that group are removed from the row.

Suit match

Same suit — clear two

When the 1st and 4th cards share the same suit — both Hearts, both Clubs — the two middle cards are removed. The outer cards remain.

The cascade. Every time cards are removed, the row collapses and new matches can form — triggering chain reactions that clear large sections at once. Win by clearing all 52 cards.

Version 2.0

More to play for

The core Railroad game is unchanged. v2 layers on progression, power-ups, and rewards that give every hand meaning beyond the final score.

Bonus round

Caboose Bonus

Trigger a cascade of three or more and you unlock the Caboose Bonus. Cards from your discards are revealed and you place them into five poker hands: Flush, Straight, Straight Flush, Full House, and Five of a Kind. Complete a hand to spin the reward wheel.

Rewards

Power Meter and Reward Wheel

Every discarded card feeds the power meter. Fill it and you spin an infinite reward wheel that grants cosmetics and power-ups. No losing segments. The meter also fills when you complete a caboose poker hand.

Power-ups

Five ways to bend the odds

Joker inserts a wild card into the deck mid-game. Derail removes two cards and sweeps the board for cascades. Meter Boost doubles your meter fill rate. Extra Pick gives you more cards in the bonus round. Switchyard rearranges your endgame.

Progression

Achievements and cosmetics

Fifteen achievements track everything from your first cascade to the impossibly rare Impossible Flush. Unlock card backs and table surfaces through the reward wheel. Meet Mac the conductor, your in-game guide and occasional gift-bearer.
